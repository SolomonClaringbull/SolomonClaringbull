Project Ideas

Overview

I have an idea to create an application that would enable up and coming musicians, managers or labels to easily get in contact with each other and share content such as videos, photos, mp3s etc via a messaging feature. This would help musicians find other musicians to collaborate with, or aid musicians in finding a manager/label or vice versa. The application’s intention is to be a social media for people in the music industry.

Motivation

As a musician, I have often found it hard to find other musicians that share the same vision or make the same music as me. Not only is it hard enough to find these kinds of people, but to find other musicians you often have to go out to music related events, such as gigs, festivals etc. This seems like a ridiculous and complicated way to find other musicians, as not only are these events not there for that purpose, but they also cost money, and even if you meet other musicians, there's a high chance they don't share the same ideas as you. I feel that there is a medium needed for other musicians to be able to contact each other so that they can easily start working on something together, as well as have the ability to get in contact with/find a manger or label to aid them in their career if their music begins to gain traction.

Description

Features of this product:

The user would have the ability to create a profile, from the choice of musician, manager or label. For a musician profile, the user would have to provide basic information to be shown on their profile such as; links to music previously made, general location, other forms of contact details (email, website, social media), a short description of music they make and some details about themselves (age, gender etc). There would be a verification process for a musician profile that would involve providing proof of music they have made (Soundcloud page, Spotify page etc) so that it filters out fake profiles, allowing only musicians who already have a musical catalogue to have a profile on the app. For a manage profile, the user would have to provide basic information to be shown on their profile such as; links to artists they manage/have managed, general location, other forms of contact details (website, social media) and a short description of who they are and their approach towards management. There would be a verification process for a manager profile, such as providing proof of the artists/musicians they have managed (such as a management contract) so that it filters out fake profiles and mangers who would otherwise be deemed untrustworthy. For a label profile, the user would have to provide basic information to be shown on their profile such as; links to/examples of artists and managers on their label, a website, the label’s social media, their general location and a short description of their label and their approach towards the music industry. There would be a verification process for a label profile, such as providing proof of the artists/musicians/mangers on their label (such as contracts) so that it filters out fake profiles and untrustworthy labels. There would be a hierarchy. This hierarchy would work as such: - Musicians can contact other musicians with no constraints and are able to send as many messages as they want. - Musicians can only contact managers/label with a limit of 2 total messages and cannot send anymore until the manager/label responds to these messages. This prevents a flood of messages from musicians trying to get a manager/label. Once the manager or label has responded to a musician’s message, both parties will have the ability to communicate freely. - Managers can freely contact any musicians they wish - Managers can contact labels but only with a limit of 2 messages and cannot send anymore until the label responds, preventing a flood of messages from managers trying to get in contact with a label. Once the label has responded to their message, both parties will have the ability to communicate freely. When a user sends a message to another user, it will automatically send a request to add the other user to their contacts. Users can also add another user without sending a message first, through the feature of an add button. When sending messages, users would have the ability to send them in the form of text, images, files, voice recordings or videos. When a user accepts messages from another user, both users will have the ability to either video call or voice call each other. All users will have to ability to block another user if they don’t wish for the user to contact them or see their profile. To search for another user, there would be a search engine where you can input names of other users or use the search engine’s selection criteria where you can select to search by musician, manager, or label. When searching using a name, the search results will display the profile/s with name. If there are no results for the inputted name, the search results will display ‘No Results’. When searching via the musician criteria, a user will have the option to refine the search with different criteria, these criteria being; genre (hip-hop, electronic etc.) and location. If these options are selected, the search results will only display musicians that relate to the selected criteria, eg. User selects to search for musician and refines the search to musicians that make hip-hop and are located around Melbourne. The search results will only display musicians that create hip-hop and are located around Melbourne. When a user chooses to search for a manager or label, they will have the option to refine their search with the criteria of location. If the user inputs location, then the search results will only display managers or labels that are related to entered criteria, eg. User selects to search for managers and refines the search to managers that are located around Melbourne. The search results will only display managers located around Melbourne. When search results have been displayed, users will also have the option to sort these results via a button that sorts the results by either relevancy to entered criteria, newest users, or oldest users, depending on how the user wishes to sort the results.
